Central Asia Public Health Measures

As the Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) continues to spread around the world, the states of Central Asia, including Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, and Uzbekistan, are all taking their own individual paths in combating the pandemic. Countries in Central Asia started to report their first COVID-19 cases in mid-March and the number of cases has risen significantly since, following a similar trend to most of the world. Most Central Asia countries have taken strong measures to protect the health and safety of their population to help contain the pandemic. These measures include; quarantines, lockdowns, remote schooling, awareness campaigns, and much more. Below is a summary of some of the public health measures that have been put into place for each Central Asia country.


Disclaimer: Data was collected from March 1st and July 31, 2020, therefore the information below may not reflect current conditions.

Kazakhstan

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), Kazakhstan was the first Country in Central Asia to report a positive COVID-19 case. Kazakhstan declared a state of emergency on March 16th after they had confirmed that they had 9 new positive COVID-19 cases between March 13th and March 15th. As it is reported, the Kazakhstan government has mandated that masks be worn in public settings, inclduing both indoor and outdoor spaces, and during public transportation. Initially kazakhstan had closed all borders to entry of non-citizens and had put air,train, and car traffic on hold, though national traffic is now resuming. As of July, 2020 many non-essential bussiness remain closed, including gyms, pools, beaches, cultural facilities, museums, religious facilities, etc... Kazakhstan had a surge of cases between the months of June and July that propmted the government to restate the importance of social distancing. The WHO reported that Kazakhstan had a total of 22,308 total COVID-19 cases at the end of June and at the end of July those numbers grew to 90,367 cases. It was also reported by several news articles that Kazakhstan was reporting a high number of pneumonia cases between the months of March and August. Several reports have claimed that the surge of pneumonia cases in Kazakhstan are likely to be COVID-19 cases. See news article here: Unknown Pneumonia is Likely COVID-19 Web Article

Kyrgyzstan

According to the Organization for Exonomic Co-operation and Devlopment (OECD), "the Kyrgyzstan government had declared a state of emergency, with some districts in quarantine and the capital, Bishkek under a nightly curfew". With social distancing measures put into place, regulations on movement and curfew are gradually being relaxed. As of August 4th, bars, clubs, playgrounds, and other entertainment facilities remain closed while resaurants, cafes, and markets have reopened. OECD also reports that all education facilities are closed until September 1st, with online enrolment in selected cities. The state of emergency was ended in May and as of July the Kyrgyzstan Government has decided not to reimpose another state of emergency over the coronavirus despite a rise in cases in some cities. At the end of July Kyrgyzstan had the highest number of COVID-19 deaths in Central Asia with a total of 1378 people.

Tajikistan

WHO reports that Tajikistan reported its first positive COVID-19 case on April 30th, 48 days after Kazakhstan's first reported case. Prior to Apil, Tajikistan hardly enforced safety precautions for COVID-19. Reports claim that the Dushanbe International Airport is closed to most commerical flights and it is not known when the airport will fully reopen. Tajikistand is restricting Tajik nationals and foreigners, including U.S. citizens, from leaving the country until travel restrictions are lifted. The tajikistan government has introduced new mandatory requirements to wear masks in public. Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan has not officially acknowledged any cases of COVID-19 within its borders. According to OECD, "the government has generally refrained from talking about the pandemic, and aside from some limited public health information, there has been little guidance to citizens or firms on preventive measures. Nevertheless, the government has taken a number of steps to prevent a COVID outbreak. It has closed its borders to non-nationals, and all non-Turkmen carriers have ceased flights to the country". The embassy reports that international commerical flights to and from Turkmenistan have been cancelled and land borders are closed to passenger traffic. Individuals are highly encouraged to wear facial masks in public settings.

Uzbekistan

Uzekbikstan has the largest population in Central Asia with a total of roughly 33 million people. Although they have the largest population, at the end of July, Uzebekistan only reported around 24,000 total positive COVID-19 cases. Uzbekistan was the second country in Central Asia to report a positive COVID-19 case. Uzbekistan announced that they had there first COVID-19 case on March 15th, 2020. OECD reports Uzebkistan began implementing national quarantine and preventative measures in March. The government closed the borders and the capital, Tashkent, was under quarantine. The government closed all non-essential businesses and have recommended that higher education be provided online. As of June 15th Uzbekistan has resumed international flights and many of the safety measures are being relaxed.
